Straight Answers

QUESTIONS?
GOOD.

Is this therapy?

No. It's coaching, not therapy/counseling/treatment. In crisis or managing a mental-health condition? Coaching isn't a substitute for professional care.

How is this different from therapy?

Therapy often focuses on healing, mental health, and emotional recovery. Coaching focuses on awareness, decision-making, accountability, and behavior change. The right fit depends on your goals.

Who's it for?

High-functioning people who know what to do but keep finding themselves back in the same patterns. Success isn't the problem. Awareness isn't the problem. The challenge is turning insight into different behavior.

What actually happens in coaching?

We identify the loop that's keeping you stuck, the trigger that starts it, and the new response you want to make automatic. Every session ends with a practical override to test in real life.

How fast?

Most people identify a pattern within the first one or two sessions. Creating lasting change takes repetition and practice over time. Some shifts happen quickly. Others take longer. Results vary.

How long do people work with you?

Most people start with a few sessions and decide from there. The goal isn't dependency. It's building a process you can use without me.

Where are you?

Online, nationwide. Everything's over video.
